About the Model

The breast cancer diagnosis predictive model is based on the VGG-19 CNN architecture.
The model classifies mammography images into 3 classes which are Benign, Malignant and Normal.
The predictive results will be shown in the form of text after users have submitted a mammography image.


Details

Dataset Used for training and testing the model:

Mammographic Image Analysis Society (MIAS) database


Accuracy of the breast cancer diagnosis predictive model: 93%

Training and testing distribution of dataset: 80:20

Used Batch Size: 64


Below is an graphical representation of the VGG-19 CNN architecture used to develop the predictive model:


By using the VGG-19 pre-trained model, we added some few layers to increase the accuracy as well as reduce the overfitting. This project utilises the VGG19 model, which is a convolutional neural network that is trained on more than a million images from the ImageNet database, as a base model. The VGG19 model was proposed by the Visual Geometry Group of Oxford University in 2014, and obtained accurate classification performance on the ImageNet dataset. On top of that, we added another 16 layers to further equip our model with the ability to classify the abnormalities present in the mammography images. Our deep learning model is outstandingly fine-tuned to produce the best accuracy possible (93%) while also optimising computing resources.
